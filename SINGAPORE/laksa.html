About Laksa

Laksa is a fragrant and spicy noodle soup that is a staple of Singaporean cuisine, blending Chinese and Malay culinary traditions. This beloved dish features a rich, creamy coconut milk-based broth infused with aromatic spices, lemongrass, and shrimp paste. It is often served with rice noodles, prawns, fish cakes, tofu puffs, and a dollop of sambal chili for an extra kick. Laksa is not just a meal but an experience that captures the heart of Singapore's multicultural food scene.

Recipe for Laksa

  1. Blend spices (lemongrass, garlic, ginger, dried chili, shrimp paste) into a paste.
  2. Saut√© the paste in oil until fragrant, then add coconut milk and chicken or prawn broth.
  3. Add fish cakes, prawns, tofu puffs, and hard-boiled eggs to the simmering broth.
  4. Cook rice noodles separately and divide them into serving bowls.
  5. Pour the flavorful broth over the noodles and toppings.
  6. Garnish with bean sprouts, fresh coriander, and lime wedges. Serve with sambal chili on the side for added heat.

Laksa is a dish that excites the palate with its spicy, savory, and creamy flavors, making it a must-try for food lovers exploring Singapore's vibrant cuisine.
